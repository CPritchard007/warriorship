The Monks of Japanese History

The Sohei

The Sohei were a type of Warrior monk that lived in the early 13th century. These monks were far from friendly, and it was because of this that many people came to fear these warriors. The armor and weapons they used shared resemblances with that of the samurai, making them fearsome in battle.

The Naginata

The main weapon that the sohei uses is the Naginata, which is a sword attached to a long pole. This weapon is used alongside with their martial arts training that allows them to make fast, and farther ranged attacks than someone with a standard sword.

The Yamabushi

The yamabushi is a large tribe of people who lived in the mountains. Unlike the sohei however, these warriors do not push to strike fear into others. These warriors spend most of there time in the mountains, where they trained in marcial arts. These warriors usually wear the same form of samurai armor, and a sword. Due to them spending most of there time in the mountains in prayer, they are believed to be quite spiritual, and some people say that the Yambushi has special powers.
